**Text:**

## Threads Platform Strategy

### Posting Frequency
- **January-February:** 3-4 posts per week
- **March (registration):** 4-5 posts per week
- **April-May:** 3-4 posts per week
- **June (event week):** Multiple posts daily

### Best Times to Post
- **Weekdays:** 9-11am EST or 1-3pm EST
- **Avoid:** Early morning (before 8am), late night
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday

### Engagement Strategy
- Respond to every reply within 6 hours
- Ask questions to encourage conversation
- Use polls/questions in posts
- Repost attendee content
- Be conversational, not promotional

### Tone Guidelines
**Do:**
- Be direct and honest
- Share real stories and examples
- Admit challenges and failures
- Use conversational language
- Ask questions and invite responses

**Don't:**
- Use hype words or oversell
- Make vague promises
- Sound corporate or promotional
- Use excessive hashtags (Threads displays them differently)
- Copy/paste identical content from other platforms

### Cross-Posting Notes
**From Bluesky to Threads:**
- Most Bluesky posts (300 chars) fit easily in Threads (500 chars)
- Can expand with additional context or examples
- Adjust tone slightly (Threads is bit more professional)

**From Threads to Instagram:**
- Threads text can become Instagram captions
- Add hashtags for Instagram (Threads uses them sparingly)
- Consider visual component for Instagram

**From Threads to LinkedIn:**
- Can expand Threads posts into longer LinkedIn posts
- Make tone more professional/formal
- Add business context or industry insights

### Hashtag Strategy for Threads
- Use sparingly (2-3 max per post)
- Threads displays hashtags in-line, not as a block
- Primary: #CJS2026 #CollaborativeJournalism
- Use topic-specific hashtags when relevant: #JournalismInnovation #SmallNewsrooms

### Character Management
- 500 character limit (vs. 300 on Bluesky)
- Use space for:
  - More context and examples
  - Longer quotes
  - Additional details
  - More conversational tone
- Don't feel need to max out every post

### Community Building
- Threads is good for ongoing conversations
- Use reply threads to go deeper on topics
- Encourage attendees to share their own stories
- Create discussion prompts
- Foster peer-to-peer connections

### Link Strategy
- Include full URLs (Threads doesn't shorten)
- Primary link: collaborativejournalism.org/cjs2026
- Use links sparingly (1 per post max)
- Links don't count against character limit
