## Email body

The CJS2026 program is taking shape — and it reflects ten years of growth, learning, and community building in collaborative journalism.

We received session pitches from newsrooms large and small, longtime collaborators and newcomers, practitioners and researchers. The result is a program that honors how far we've come while tackling the challenges ahead.

### Programming themes

**Monday, June 8: Reflecting and connecting**
- Opening keynote on collaborative journalism's evolution from experiment to ecosystem
- Lightning talks showcasing innovative projects from the past year
- Case studies from veteran collaborators and first-time partnerships
- Networking sessions and collaborative brainstorming
- Evening celebration marking ten years of CJS

**Tuesday, June 9: Learning and doing**
- Hands-on workshops covering editorial, operational, and technical aspects of collaboration
- Deep-dive sessions on sustainability, funding, and partnership models
- Problem-solving discussions tackling shared challenges
- Transition programming for those continuing to INN Days (June 9–11)

### Topics you'll explore

- Building and sustaining collaborative newsroom partnerships
- Managing multi-organization investigations and projects
- Collaborative approaches to coverage of critical issues
- Technology and tools for distributed teams
- Business models and funding strategies for collaborative work
- Equity and inclusion in collaborative journalism
- Measuring impact and demonstrating value to stakeholders

### Who you'll meet

This year we're expecting 130–150 attendees including:

- Journalists from collaborative newsrooms and networks
- Editors and newsroom leaders managing partnerships
- Funders supporting collaborative journalism initiatives
- Researchers studying collaborative models and impact
- Technologists building tools for collaborative work

### What's next

**Registration opens in March** with early bird pricing for the first 50 attendees.

The full schedule will be published in April. In the meantime, you can start planning your trip to Chapel Hill.
