# CJS2026 Promotional Release Calendar
## January 2026 - June 2026

**Event:** Collaborative Journalism Summit 2026 (10th Anniversary)
**Dates:** June 8-9, 2026
**Location:** UNC Friday Center, Chapel Hill, NC
**Theme:** "From experiment to ecosystem"
**Tagline:** "Prepare to partner"

---

## Strategic Overview

### Campaign Goals

**Primary objectives:**
1. Drive 130-150 registrations by May 2026
2. Generate 200+ session pitch submissions by January 31
3. Build momentum around 10-year milestone and ecosystem growth narrative
4. Showcase value of co-location with INN Days (June 9-11)
5. Engage alumni network across 9 previous summits

**Audience segments:**
- **Alumni** (2017-2025 attendees): Nostalgia + evolution narrative
- **First-timers**: Value proposition + partnership opportunities
- **Speakers/presenters**: Thought leadership platform
- **Funders**: ROI stories + Knight Foundation partnership
- **Journalists**: Practical collaboration skills + networking

**Success metrics:**
- Email open rates >30%, click-through >8%
- Social engagement up 25% YoY
- Session pitch quality (diverse topics, experienced presenters)
- Early bird conversion rate >40%

### Coordination Tactics

**Announcement Days (Schedule, Early Bird, etc.):**
1. Email goes out first (morning)
2. Medium post published (midday)
3. Social posts staggered throughout day:
   - LinkedIn (morning, professionals checking before work)
   - Twitter/X (mid-morning, thread)
   - Instagram (lunch, peak engagement)
   - Bluesky (afternoon)
   - Threads (evening)

**Speaker Spotlights:**
- Coordinate with speakers for content (quotes, photos)
- Tag speakers' accounts across platforms
- Ask speakers to share/repost
- Sequence: LinkedIn announcement → Twitter thread → Instagram visual

**User-Generated Content:**
- Encourage attendees to share registration confirmations
- Repost attendee content leading up to event
- Create shareable graphics (badges, countdowns)
- Feature attendee stories in email/social

**Alumni Engagement:**
- Tag/mention past attendees in anniversary content
- Ask for testimonials throughout campaign
- Create "Where are they now?" content series
- Encourage past attendees to recruit newcomers

### Weekly Rhythm (March-May)

**Mondays:** Social engagement (questions, polls, community building)
**Wednesdays:** Content depth (Medium posts, speaker spotlights, threads)
**Fridays:** Email newsletters (every other week) + social highlights
**Weekends:** Lighter social content, behind-the-scenes, personal notes

## Success Tracking

### Metrics to Monitor Weekly

**Email:**
- Open rate (target: >30%)
- Click-through rate (target: >8%)
- Unsubscribe rate (keep <0.5%)
- Conversion to registration

**Social:**
- Engagement rate (likes, comments, shares)
- Follower growth
- Click-throughs to registration
- Hashtag usage

**Website:**
- Traffic to summit.collaborativejournalism.org
- Session pitch submissions
- Registration conversions
- Time on site

### Adjustments Based on Performance

**If email open rates drop:**
- Test subject lines (more specific vs. more curious)
- Segment list (alumni vs. new)
- Adjust send times

**If social engagement is low:**
- Increase visual content
- More interactive posts (polls, questions)
- Leverage speaker/attendee networks

**If registration lags:**
- Add urgency (early bird deadline, limited spots)
- Testimonials from past attendees
- Clearer value proposition
- Payment plan options
