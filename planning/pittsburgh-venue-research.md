# Pittsburgh Venue & Partnership Research
## Collaborative Journalism Summit 2026

*Research compiled: January 4, 2026*
*Event dates: June 8-9, 2026*

---

## Executive Summary

This document identifies Pittsburgh media organizations, journalism education centers, foundations, and event venues that could partner with or host the Collaborative Journalism Summit 2026. The Center for Cooperative Media (Montclair State University) organizes this annual summit celebrating collaborative reporting efforts globally.

### Top Venue Recommendations

1. **Point Park University Center for Media Innovation** - Strongest journalism connection, proven track record with Newsapalooza
2. **Alloy 26** - Largest coworking space (50,000 sq ft, 300+ capacity)
3. **Senator John Heinz History Center** - Large capacity (up to 3,500), professional facilities
4. **Carnegie Mellon University** - Academic prestige, unique spaces

## 5. Major Event Venues

### Senator John Heinz History Center
**TOP VENUE RECOMMENDATION - LARGE CAPACITY**

**Location:** Strip District, Pittsburgh
**Status:** Smithsonian affiliate, largest history museum in Pennsylvania
**Parking:** Large public lot across the street, valet available

**Full Facility Rental:** Entire museum, all exhibits, all floors
**Capacity Range:** 10-1,000 guests (up to 3,500 for full facility)

**Event Spaces:**

**Great Hall (1st Floor):**
- Former Chautauqua Lake Ice House
- Two-story staircase
- High vaulted brick ceilings
- Original steel beams
- Ceremonies: up to 325 guests
- Cocktail hours: up to 350 guests
- Wedding receptions: up to 200 guests (with dance floor)

**Mueller Center (5th Floor):**
- Contemporary ballroom
- Wall of windows
- 15' x 135' outdoor deck
- Gorgeous river and downtown skyline views

**Detre Library & Archives (6th Floor):**
- Conference room with brick walls
- Reproductions of paintings from collection
- Quiet breakout session space
- Meetings: 10-12 people
- Also used as wedding party dressing room

**U.S. Steel Foundation Boardroom (7th Floor):**
- Brick walls, hardwood floors
- Incredible downtown Pittsburgh views through window wall

**3rd Floor Discovery Multipurpose Room:**
- 60-120 guests seated banquet style
- 150 for cocktail reception or theater-style seating

**Services:**
- Common Plea Catering (exclusive)
- Competitive rental rates
- Professional staff
- Revenue benefits History Center exhibitions, programs, collections preservation

**Event Types:** Weddings, meetings, brunches, galas, fundraisers, birthday parties, cocktail receptions, dinner parties

**Past Journalism Events:** Pittsburgh Post-Gazette panel discussions

**Partnership Potential:** Very high - professional facilities, large capacity, proven event hosting

## Recommendations Summary

### Tier 1: Strongest Journalism Connection + Adequate Capacity

1. **Point Park University Center for Media Innovation**
   - Proven journalism track record (Newsapalooza)
   - Pittsburgh Media Partnership hub
   - Built-in journalist network (34 member outlets)
   - Smaller space (4,000 sq ft) - best for breakout sessions or as secondary venue
   - Contact: Tara Maziarz-Myers (tmyers@pointpark.edu)

### Tier 2: Best Capacity + Professional Facilities

2. **Alloy 26**
   - Largest coworking space (50,000 sq ft)
   - Auditorium (150), Lobby (large networking), conference room
   - Modern, purpose-built for events
   - Near WQED (PBS Pittsburgh)

3. **Senator John Heinz History Center**
   - Professional event operations
   - Scalable capacity (10-3,500)
   - Multiple rooms for concurrent sessions
   - Catering included
   - Prior journalism event hosting (Post-Gazette panels)

### Tier 3: Academic Prestige

4. **Carnegie Mellon University**
   - Multiple unique spaces (Singleton Room, Posner Center, CFA venues)
   - Full conference services (40+ years experience)
   - Journalism programs (#319 nationally)

5. **University of Pittsburgh**
   - Iconic Cathedral of Learning (Cathy Commons, Babcock Room, Nationality Rooms)
   - University Club (state-of-the-art facilities)
   - Conference Services manages bookings

### Recommended Partnership Strategy

**Hybrid Approach:**
- **Primary Venue:** Alloy 26 or Heinz History Center (plenary sessions, large gatherings)
- **Partner Organization:** Point Park University Center for Media Innovation (content partnership, session hosting, journalist network)
- **Secondary Venues:** CMI space, University venues (breakout sessions, receptions)

**Key Contacts to Pursue:**
1. Tara Maziarz-Myers (Point Park CMI): tmyers@pointpark.edu
2. Andrew Conte, Ph.D. (CMI Director, Pittsburgh Media Partnership)
