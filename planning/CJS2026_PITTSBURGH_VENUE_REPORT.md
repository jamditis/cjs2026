# CJS2026 Pittsburgh venue research report

**Collaborative Journalism Summit 2026 - 10th anniversary**
**Dates:** June 8-9, 2026 | **Location:** Pittsburgh, PA | **Expected attendance:** 200-400

---

## Executive summary

Pittsburgh offers excellent venue options for the 10th anniversary Collaborative Journalism Summit. Based on comprehensive research across universities, conference centers, media organizations, and logistics, this report identifies the top venue recommendations aligned with CJS's history of partnering with journalism schools and media organizations.

### Top 3 venue recommendations

| Rank | Venue | Type | Capacity | Why it's ideal |
|------|-------|------|----------|----------------|
| **1** | **Point Park University CMI + Pittsburgh Playhouse** | University + Theater | 200-400+ | Strongest journalism connection; hosts Newsapalooza; Pittsburgh Media Partnership hub; 34 local news outlets in network |
| **2** | **Duquesne University Power Center** | University | 400+ | Excellent media/journalism program; Power Center ballroom + breakout rooms; between Oakland and downtown |
| **3** | **Senator John Heinz History Center** | Cultural/Museum | 10-1,000 | Professional facilities; hosted Post-Gazette journalism panels; unique Pittsburgh heritage atmosphere |

### Strategic recommendation

**Hybrid partnership model** (mirrors CJS 2019 Philadelphia approach):
- **Primary venue:** Point Park CMI + Pittsburgh Playhouse (plenary sessions)
- **Content partner:** Pittsburgh Media Partnership (built-in 34-outlet journalist network)
- **Evening reception:** Heinz History Center or Strip District venue (Primanti Bros. + pierogies)
- **Hotel block:** Downtown cluster (Wyndham Grand, Drury Plaza, Westin)

### Key patterns

1. **University partnerships dominate** - 6 of 8 in-person summits used universities
2. **Journalism/media school preference** - Venues with communications departments preferred
3. **Capacity range:** 150-300 typical, venues capable of 400-600
4. **Multiple breakout spaces essential** - 5+ rooms for concurrent sessions
5. **Media organization partnerships** - Add authenticity and networking value
6. **Professional AV/broadcast capabilities** - Recording and streaming standard

- **Temperature:** 59-81°F (excellent conference weather)
- **Sunshine:** 8 hours/day
- **Rainfall:** 9-12 rainy days
- **Verdict:** Ideal for outdoor networking

### Summer university availability

**Critical finding:** June 8-9, 2026 falls **between Pitt Summer Sessions I & II**
- Pitt Session I ends June 12
- Pitt Session II begins June 15
- **Result:** Lower campus activity = excellent venue availability
