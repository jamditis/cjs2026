# Pittsburgh University Venue Research for CJS2026

**Conference dates:** June 8-9, 2026
**Expected attendance:** 200-400 journalism professionals
**Research date:** January 4, 2026

---

## Executive summary

Five major Pittsburgh universities offer suitable conference facilities for CJS2026, with strong journalism/communications programs and excellent accessibility via public transit. Key findings:

- **Summer availability:** June 8-9, 2026 falls during summer session at most universities, with lower campus activity
- **Transit:** All Oakland-area universities (Pitt, CMU, Duquesne) are accessible via Port Authority buses and the 28X Airport Flyer
- **Journalism programs:** Point Park has the strongest dedicated journalism program; Duquesne has excellent media facilities
- **Pricing:** Specific rates require direct quotes; external event pricing typically higher than academic/non-profit rates

## 2. Carnegie Mellon University (Oakland)

### Location & accessibility
- **Address:** Oakland neighborhood, Pittsburgh, PA
- **Transit:** Port Authority buses (free for CMU students/staff with semester fee); 28X Airport Flyer stops at campus
- **Hotels nearby:** Same Oakland hotel cluster as Pitt
- **Accessibility:** ADA-compliant transit; campus shuttle to Oakland, Downtown, Lawrenceville
- **Shuttle:** Free CMU shuttle within 3-mile radius

### Conference facilities

**Cohon University Center** (primary venue)

**Rangos Ballroom**
- **Capacity:** Up to 450 guests
- **Divisible:** Can split into Rangos 1, 2, and 3 for smaller events
- **Stage:** Large performance stage (22' x 36')
- **Projection:** Large screen, front or side projection
- **Features:** Flexible for lectures, banquets, dances

**Simmons Auditorium**
- **Size:** 5,600 sq ft flexible event space
- **Capacity:** 232 retractable bleacher seats + 300 auditorium-style chairs (total ~530)
- **Features:** Soundproof airwall to divide into two spaces
- **Lobby:** Todd Kniola Lobby for receptions
- **Uses:** Conferences, banquets, large meetings, social gatherings

**Conference rooms**
- **Caffee Room:** 12-person, overlooks the Cut
- **Class of 1987:** Conference room with projector and screen
- **Connan Room:** Large multipurpose, first floor near Wean Commons
- **McKenna, Peter, Wright:** Divisible into three rooms or one large space; all have projector, screen, audio
- **Pake Boardroom:** Small boardroom, 16 guests
- **Danforth Conference Room:** Available for smaller meetings

### Academic programs
- **No dedicated journalism school** (0 bachelor's degrees awarded in 2021-22)
- **English Department:** Writing & Communication, Professional Writing, Rhetoric programs
- **Global Communication & Applied Translation MA**
- **Washington Semester Program:** Journalism internships (C-SPAN, NPR, PBS, Politico, Washington Post, Vox)
- **Ranking:** #319 journalism school nationally (out of 1,736)

### Services & amenities
- **Catering:** Full catering guide available for university affiliates
- **AV:** Partnership with MediaTech Department; microphone rental to video camera operation
- **Media Services Hours:** Mon-Thu 8am-10pm, Fri 8am-5pm (weekend requests by Wed 5pm)
- **Tech:** Standard furniture and amenities included; sign stands, flip charts, stanchions at no cost
- **Internet:** Free coordination for external attendees
- **Additional:** Can work with external AV companies for off-campus or specialized needs

### Summer 2026 calendar
- **March 10:** Summer 2026 registration opens
- **May 23:** Summer All add/audit/tuition adjustment drop deadline
- **June 8-9:** CJS2026 dates (during summer session)
- **June 19:** Juneteenth (University closed)
- **July 2:** Summer All pass/no pass & withdrawal deadline
- **July 3:** Independence Day Observed (University closed)
- **July 30:** Last day of summer classes (Session All, Two, Mini-6)

### Pricing & rental
- **External events:** Must complete external event request form for quote
- **Restrictions:** On-campus event space for external events limited to dates outside academic year
- **Philosophy:** All events must support educational goals of university
- **Note:** Specific pricing rates not published; requires direct inquiry

### Contact information
- **Contact page:** [cmu.edu/conferences/contact](https://www.cmu.edu/conferences/contact/index.html)
- **Event request:** Internal and external forms available on website
- **Website:** [cmu.edu/conferences](https://www.cmu.edu/conferences/)

### Pros
- Excellent facilities with state-of-the-art technology
- Rangos Ballroom ideal for 450-person capacity
- Simmons Auditorium perfect for plenary sessions
- One-stop-shop service (40+ years combined experience)
- Strong tech/media infrastructure

### Cons
- Limited external event availability (outside academic year preferred)
- No specific pricing published
- Weak journalism program (more tech/engineering focused)
- All events must support university educational goals

## Key considerations for venue selection

### Academic fit
- **Duquesne** and **Point Park** have the strongest journalism/media programs
- Hosting at a journalism school creates natural partnerships and student engagement opportunities
- Student media infrastructure (radio, TV, newspapers) could provide coverage and documentation

### Capacity & configuration
- **200-400 attendees** requires flexible spaces
- Need: 1 large plenary space (400+) + multiple breakout rooms (50-100 each)
- **Duquesne Power Center** and **CMU Rangos** best fit this model
- **Pitt Connolly Ballroom** also suitable but requires multiple buildings for breakouts

### Location & logistics
- **Oakland universities** (Pitt, CMU, Duquesne): Clustered together, excellent transit, multiple hotels
- **Point Park downtown**: Urban setting, walking to restaurants/nightlife, different atmosphere
- **Transit:** All Oakland + downtown options have 28X Airport Flyer access
- **Hotels:** Oakland has 3-4 university-adjacent hotels; downtown has 15+ options

### Summer scheduling
- **June 8-9, 2026** falls during summer sessions at all universities
- **Lower campus activity** = better availability
- **CMU restriction:** External events preferred outside academic year (may be issue)
- **June 19:** Juneteenth (all universities closed) â€” not a conflict for June 8-9

### Budget considerations
- **No public pricing** for any venue (all require quotes)
- **External event fees** apply to non-university groups
- **Non-profit rates:** May be available; ask specifically
- **Compare quotes:** Contact all top-tier options for competitive pricing

### Journalism summit theme alignment
- **Center for Media Innovation (Point Park):** Glass studios, newsroom, visual media hub aesthetic
- **CEIM at Duquesne:** Professional collaboration space, student media outlets
- **Pitt Media & Professional Communications:** Hands-on experience, corporate/media company internships
- **Sustainability angle (Chatham):** If environmental journalism is a theme, Eden Hall could be a workshop location
