# Pittsburgh Venue Research for CJS2026
## Journalism Summit, June 8-9, 2026 | 200-400 Attendees

**Research Date:** January 4, 2026
**Event Type:** 2-day journalism conference with plenary sessions and breakout rooms
**Requirements:** Professional quality, cost-effective, 4-6 breakout rooms, AV infrastructure, accessible

---

## Executive Summary

Pittsburgh offers a strong mix of affordable, professional conference venues suitable for a journalism summit. Key findings:

- **Best Overall Value:** David L. Lawrence Convention Center (smaller spaces), Wyndham Grand Pittsburgh Downtown
- **Most Unique/Memorable:** Heinz History Center, Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Hall, August Wilson Cultural Center
- **Most Budget-Friendly:** Energy Innovation Center, Carnegie Library venues, Comfort Inn Conference Center
- **Best Downtown Access:** All downtown hotels and Convention Center have excellent public transit access

All venues researched are accessible via Pittsburgh Regional Transit (bus and light rail), with ADA-compliant facilities.

## 2. Convention/Conference Centers

### David L. Lawrence Convention Center
**Location:** 1000 Fort Duquesne Blvd, Pittsburgh, PA 15222 (Downtown)
**Website:** [pittsburghcc.com](https://www.pittsburghcc.com/)

**Spaces & Capacities:**
- **53 meeting rooms total** (including 2 lecture halls with 250 seats each)
- **Spirit of Pittsburgh Ballroom:** 31,610 sq ft
- **Total exhibit space:** 313,400 sq ft (236,900 sq ft column-free)
- **South Terrace Green Roof:** 20,000 sq ft programmable outdoor event space
- **Overall capacity:** Up to 109,445 people (varies by configuration)

**Rental Rates:**
- 2024/2025: $2,900 - $29,432 per event
- 2026/2027: $3,150 - $30,059 per event
- Note: Rates set well into future for predictable budgeting

**Catering:** Levy Convention Centers (exclusive caterer for all food/beverage)

**AV/Technology:** State-of-the-art facilities

**Accessibility:**
- **Parking:** 700-car garage (7'8" clearance)
  - First level: 5 ADA spots (lobby/elevator area) + 8 ADA spots (East Entrance)
  - Second level: 4 ADA spots (by elevator)
  - ADA push-button door openers on entrance/exit doors
- Complimentary non-motorized wheelchairs (first-come, first-served) at East Lobby, West Lobby, Spirit Ballroom security desks
- Hotel walkway access from garage (floors 1-2) or DLCC 3rd floor with automatic doors
- Dedicated evacuation plan for guests with disabilities

**Transit Access:**
- **Subway:** Within 5 blocks of Wood Street and Penn Station stops
- **Highways:** I-579, I-279, I-376 exits
- **Airport:** 28X Airport Flyer bus runs every 30-35 minutes to Liberty Avenue (few blocks away)

**Non-Profit Rates:** Not specified in public materials; contact for quote

**Best For:** Large journalism summits requiring extensive breakout space, prestigious downtown location, organizations prioritizing sustainability (LEED Gold certified)

**Contact:**
- Lori Novacek, Booking Coordinator: 412-325-6161 or lnovacek@pittsburghcc.com
- Chad Ford (Green Roof rentals): 412-325-6158 or cford@pittsburghcc.com
- ADA Coordinator Chris Doughty: 412-325-6135 or DLCC-ADACoordinator@pittsburghcc.com

**Awards:** LEED Gold certification (environmental stewardship), top venue for large conferences

### General Pittsburgh Venue Pricing

**Average Hourly Rates:**
- Meeting rooms: $97/hour (range: $75-$118/hour)
- Event venues: $110/hour (smaller: $136/hour, larger: $155/hour)
- 1-hour bookings typically: $24 to $500

**Group Hotel Rates:**
- Average 3-star group rate (past year): $149.37

**Convention Center Rates:**
- 2026/2027: $3,150 - $30,059 per event (capacity-dependent)

---

### Public Transit & Accessibility

**Pittsburgh Regional Transit (PRT):**
- ADA-compliant buses, light rail (T), Monongahela Incline
- All stations, vehicles, and Incline are ADA-compliant
- Bus lifts/ramps and kneelers on most buses

**Specialized Accessible Transportation:**
- VETaxi and ZTrip offer accessible vehicles

**David L. Lawrence Convention Center Transit:**
- Within 5 blocks of Wood Street and Penn Station (subway)
- 28X Airport Flyer bus (every 30-35 minutes) stops on Liberty Avenue
- I-579, I-279, I-376 highway access

**Downtown Hotel Access:**
- All major downtown hotels within walking distance of public transit
- Most offer valet or nearby parking garages

**ADA Parking Standards:**
- 1 accessible space per 25 total parking spots
- 1 in 6 accessible spaces must be van-accessible
- Spaces must be 96" wide with 96" adjacent access lane

## Recommendations by Budget & Priority

### Best Overall Value (Large Event: 300-400 attendees)
1. **David L. Lawrence Convention Center** - Smaller spaces, excellent facilities, established rates
2. **Wyndham Grand Pittsburgh Downtown** - Most meeting space of any Pittsburgh hotel, river views
3. **Heinz History Center** - Unique atmosphere, competitive rates, excellent catering

### Best for Mid-Sized Events (200-300 attendees)
1. **Heinz History Center** - Multiple breakout rooms, Pittsburgh heritage connection
2. **Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Hall** - Historic venue, accommodates 20-2,300 guests
3. **Energy Innovation Center** - Modern, innovation-focused, good AV infrastructure

### Most Budget-Friendly
1. **Carnegie Library venues** - Free/low-cost meeting rooms, some large spaces available
2. **Energy Innovation Center** - ~$5,500-$8,000 for weekend (2019 rates)
3. **Comfort Inn Conference Center** - Suburban, affordable, 10,000 sq ft space

### Most Memorable/Unique Atmosphere
1. **Heinz History Center** - Great Hall with vintage vehicles, historical significance
2. **August Wilson Cultural Center** - Cultural programming opportunities, 492-seat theater
3. **Soldiers & Sailors Memorial Hall** - Grand ballroom, military history, Oakland location
4. **The Andy Warhol Museum** - Contemporary arts atmosphere, Pittsburgh cultural icon

### Best for Staying On-Site
1. **Omni William Penn Hotel** - Historic elegance, 597 rooms, excellent group rates
2. **Wyndham Grand Pittsburgh Downtown** - Largest hotel meeting space, 35 event rooms
3. **DoubleTree by Hilton Pittsburgh Downtown** - 342 rooms, 12,000 sq ft meeting space

### Best Downtown Access & Transit
1. **David L. Lawrence Convention Center** - 5 blocks from subway, Airport Flyer access
2. **Omni William Penn Hotel** - Central business district, walkable to everything
3. **Wyndham Grand Pittsburgh Downtown** - Central location, river views
